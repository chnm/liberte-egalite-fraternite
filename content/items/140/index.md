From an English periodical of 1819, this antirevolutionary print portrays the <em>sans–culottes</em> as drunkards anxious to destroy by fire, gallows, and guillotine rather than to work for their own good. The image satirizes the idea of <em>sans–culotte</em> simplicity by arranging the two figures and the guillotine as an aristocratic coat of arms.
